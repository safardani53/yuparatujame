The hidden weapon that all celebs are using to keep boobs perky on the red carpet The Sun

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Friday, April 26, 2024

WHEN Vicky Pattison posed on the red carpet at the NTAs in a plunging strapless white gown, all eyes were on her.

The former reality star ditched the care-free vibes of her Geordie Shore days in favour of a classier look - and her cleavage was the envy of every woman in town.

Luckily for us, the 32-year-old shared her secrets with the word the next day - admitting she's used Perky Pairs breast tape patches to get a natural-looking boob lift.

And she's not alone.

Reality stars Gemma Collins, Olivia Buckland and Chloe Simms have all ditched the bras in favour of Perky Pears lift and shape tape in recent months, while fellow Geordie Shore babe Marnie Simpson even put the sticky pads to the test to support her expanding boobs during pregnancy.

Holly Willoughby’s stylist Angie Smith even stocks up on the brand’s products - although the This Morning host has previously denied using them herself.

Perky Pears have become popular with celebs in recent years after stars like Kim Kardashian admitted she was forced to use gaffer tape to hoist up her double Ds.

Other stars who have used normal tape have - including Beyonce and Cheryl - have later suffered wardrobe malfunctions.

Perky Pear founder Samantha Ryder says: "Celebrity stylists have plenty of tips and tricks up their sleeves to ensure their clients exude glam on the red carpet, and we would estimate that as many as 80 per cent of female celebrities on any red carpet are taped up to some extent.

"At this year’s NTAs we reckon many of the stars will have been sporting our product or other breast tape options, with Susanna Reid, Toff, Jesy Nelson and Jaqueline Jossa all wearing styles that may have benefited from a secret uplift.

"In the ultra-competitive celebrity world, red carpet looks are becoming more daring and HD cameras pick up every detail so tape alternatives that offer picture perfection every time are essential."

Millionaire pair behind Perky Pear

Samantha Ryder founded the firm with her twin sister Charlotte in 2015 after struggling to find breast tape or adhesive bras for her size E cup.

Then 24, the twin sisters designed their own tape that could cope with heavier breasts with a single application - eventually launching their Perky Pear Lift & Shape Tape in 2017.

They now have several styles and shapes in the the range, in sizes from A-G, which retail at £12.99 to 17.99. And it's become a thriving business with a £1million turnover.

“I developed the product by finding a comfortable, lightweight and breathable material which we perfected into the current pear-shaped design that lifts and shapes the breast, delivering results similar to a surgical breast lift," says Samantha.

“We have a wide variety of celebrity fans that use the product, including Vicky Pattison who is one of our most prolific superfans and who has loved the product from the first time she used them."

Josie Gibson recently showed the difference she saw when using Perky Pears by posing with one on and one off - while Gabby Allen proved the stickers help women with smaller boobs too.

How does it work?

Essentially, long strips of tape, around 12 inches, are applied to the outside of each breast and up to the shoulder.

Avoiding the nipple is advisable to avoid pain when removing.

With the £17.99 Perky Pear tape that Vicky used on the red carpet last week, which works with strapless dresses, the shape is pre-cut with slanted sides to keep it hidden in plunging styles.

The cotton material makes it breathable - avoiding sweaty itchy patches the old-style gaffer tape left you with.

The wearer lifts her boob to the desired position and places the rounded edge underneath for support, before smoothing the tape on to the sides of each boob.
