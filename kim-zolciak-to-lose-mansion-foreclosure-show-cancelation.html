Kim Zolciak to lose $2.6M mansion to foreclosure after she failed to pay back $300K loan follo

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Sunday, July 28, 2024

KIM Zolciak-Biermann is set to lose her $2.6M mansion in Georgia to foreclosure after Bravo canceled her show Don't Be Tardy.

Legal documents obtained by The U.S. Sun show that the star and her ex-NFL star husband Kroy Biermann "failed to pay back" a $300K loan after the show ended.

A Notice of Sale Under Power confirmed that Kim, 44, and Kroy, 37, had their five-bedroom, 6.5-bathroom home foreclosed on and is being put up for sale.

According to the document, the sprawling 6,907-square-foot home "will be sold at public outcry to the highest bidder for cash before the courthouse door of Fulton County, Georgia."

Kim and Kroy's home was foreclosed upon "because of, among other possible events of default, failure to pay the indebtedness."

The couple took out a $300K loan against the home and, according to the documents, failed to pay it back.

More Bravo News

Per legal documents, Kim and Kroy purchased the home in December 2013.

The initial Notice of Sale Under Power was published on October 6.

Kim and Kroy's family home will go on sale "on the first Tuesday in November, 2022," according to the legal docs.

The Real Housewives Of Atlanta alum did not immediately respond to The U.S. Sun's request for comment.

KIM'S HOME

According to a real estate listing, Kim and Kroy's family home is valued at an estimated $2, 535,285.

It has five bedrooms and 6.5 bathrooms and boasts 6,907 square feet of space.

The home was sold for $880,000 in 2012.

Their mansion is situated in the Manor Golf & Country Club property on the 18th hole near a lake.

There is a main bedroom on the main level and four additional bedrooms upstairs.

The home has a large finished basement, a six-car garage, and an in-ground pool.

Ariana was charged with misdemeanor DUI, misdemeanor improper/erratic lane change, and underage alcohol possession/purchase of alcohol.
