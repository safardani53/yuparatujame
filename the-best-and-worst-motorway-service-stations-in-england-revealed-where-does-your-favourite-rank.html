The best and worst motorway service stations in England revealed - where does your favourite rank?

Posted by Martina Birk on Monday, May 6, 2024

FORGET Alan Partridge, this is the definitive list of motorway service stations in England.

The comedy legend who famously had an encyclopaedic knowledge of stop-offs has been beaten by this ultimate rundown.

And you'll want to avoid Heston on the eastbound M4 which has been named as the worst place to stop for a comfort break.

Reviewed as "pretty rank", "dirty" and "disgusting", the service station has a customer satisfaction score of just 62 per cent.

The results were revealed by Transport Focus which quizzed 8,700 drivers on food, toilets, staff and car parking at 112 sites in England.

While Heston - which turns 50 next year - was slammed, the M4's Reading Westbound services was crowned top of the pit stops with a 100 per cent score.

Moto, which operates Heston and four other of the worst sites - but also Reading, said: "We're disappointed that the visit to Heston East was done in the middle of building works, although we recognise that in this instance we haven't done as well as we should have done.

"It's only fair to point out that Heston East is one of the smallest service areas on the motorway network and so is unable to provide many of the facilities we have at our larger sites."

Across the country, 90 per cent of customers were happy with service station visits with the majority pleased with toilet cleanliness and friend staff.

However, a third felt they were getting ripped off for food and drink.

Half of drivers bought refreshments during a break but only 64 per cent rated it as value for money.

We previously revealed how drivers pay a "snack tax" with soft drinks, sandwiches, crisps and chocolate costing three times as much compared to supermarkets.

Services in the south west were found to be worst charging £15.78 for a basket of snacks - when you can pick them up for £6.11 elsewhere.

The worst service stations in England

1. Heston Services Eastbound (Moto) - M4

2. Stafford Services Southbound (Roadchef) - M6

3. Rownhams Services Southbound (Roadchef) - M27

4. Rownhams Services Northbound (Roadchef) - M27

5. Toddington Services Southbound (Moto) - M1

6. Washington Services Northbound (Moto) - A1 (M)

7. Winchester Services Southbound (Moto) - M3

8. Membury Services Eastbound (Welcome Break) - M4

9. Clacket Lane Services Eastbound (Roadchef) - M25

10. Bridgwater Services (Moto) - M5

Anthony Smith, chief executive of the independent watchdog Transport Focus, said: "Ensuring that visitors feel their experience is worth the money paid, that the choice of food is good and the services are welcoming and well-maintained are important areas for motorway service operators to watch."

And Transport Minister John Hayes added: "Motorways services can and should be lovely places for drivers to enjoy not just places they have to stop.

"Congratulations to those scoring the most highly. They show what can be done when what motorists want and deserve drives what is on offer.

"Now I want to go much further to raise and meet those expectations.

The best service stations in England

  • Reading Services Westbound (Moto) – M4
  • Rivington Services Northbound (EuroGarages) – M61
  • Corley Services Northbound (Welcome Break) – M6
  • Gloucester Services Southbound (Westmorland) – M5
  • Gloucester Services Northbound (Westmorland)
  • Knutsford Services Southbound (Moto) – M6
  • Norton Canes Services (Roadchef) – M6 Toll
  • Tebay Services Northbound (Westmorland) – M6
  • Tebay Services Southbound (Westmorland) – M6
  • Cobham Services (Extra) – M25

    • "My new taskforce will bring together industry and independent experts to look at how services can be fit for future motorists and reflect the particularity of localities which host these important facilities.

    "Thoughtful design and a range of goods and services can make all motorway services as good as the best."
