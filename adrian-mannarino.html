Although Adrian Mannarino has given only a few eye-catching performances and worthwhile seriousness in games, he is still well-known on the tennis court.

Mannarino is a left-handed professional tennis player who started his journey in 2004. As of 2024, he ranks the world No. 46 in the ATP singles rankings, while his highest rank is world No. 22. 

Adrian claimed his first ATP singles title at the 2019 Rosmalen Open. He is currently the highest-ranked French tennis player. 

The French tennis player won his first tour-level on October 7, 2022, at the Astana Open, playing alongside Fabrice Martin to beat Polish tennis players Hubert Hurkaacz and Jan Zielinski at 2-6, 6-2, and 10-5 set. 

The player is the all-surface player who plays under the coach, Erwan Tortuyaux.

Adrian Mannarino | Early Life 

Adrian Mannarino was born on 29 June 1988, under Cancer’s sun sign, to his parents, Annie and Florent Mannarino. His father, Florent, is a tennis player, while his mother, Annie, is a school teacher.

The French tennis player is the second youngest of five children. He has three older brothers: Julien Mannarino, Morgan Mannarino, and Thomas Mannarino. At the same time, he has a younger sister named Iris Mannarino.

In addition, he was born in Soisy-sous-Montmorency, France, a commune in the Val-d’Oise département located 15.3 km (9.5 mi) north of Paris, France.

Not to mention, he grew up in that same town and spent his childhood days there. Mannarino hasn’t updated or, let’s say, publicized his high school and college education. 

Adrian Mannarino | Career 

Like most athletes, Mannarino started early in the field of tennis at five.

Back then, his father used to teach him gameplay, and his older brother also played the sport. As he started training for tennis, he developed his love for the game.

Adrian played in his hometown tennis clubs and then partook in the local games. Moreover, his parents used to drive him to the court for his matches. 

He has admired left-handed players like Rios, Ivanisevic, and Henri Leconte since his youth and dreamt of making a career just like them. 

Although Mannarino started his professional career in 2004, his Grand Slam singles debut only came at the 2007 French Open. Unfortunately, he lost his debut match over Marin Čilić in straight sets.

French Open 

Adrian got a yellow card for the singles main draw of the Grand Slam Tournament in 2008, where he lost to Argentine Diego Junqueira in the first round in straight sets. 

Later, he competed in the 2008 French Open men’s doubles, which also gave him a loss in the first round.

Mannarino played in the 2008 Open de Moselle in France, where he advanced to the semi-finals, defeating Andreas Seppi, Rik De Voest, and Marc Gicquel before losing to Paul-Hanri Mathieu in the second round. 

Likewise, he bagged a single event in an ATP Challenger Tour in New Jersey, defeating Andreas Beck in two tiebreaks in the final. 

The professional tennis player appeared in the inaugural Masters of France. He lost three round-robin matches in a straight set to Paul-Henri Mathieu, Michaël Llodra, and Arnaud Clément.

Australian Open 

Mannarino first partook in the Australian Open in 2009, a loss against Fernando Verdasco in the first round. 

 In 2011, he was defeated by six-time champion Roger Federer in straight sets at the Australian Open. 

Jumping straight to 2016, his Australian Open was paired with Lucas Pouille, as they defeated three seeded pairs in the quarterfinals that year. Unfortunately, they lost to Jamie Murray and Bruno Soares in the semi-finals. 

Seeded 26th at the 2018 Australian Open, Mannarino reached the singles main draw third round for the first time in his career but lost to Dominic Thiem in straight sets. 

Wimbledon Open 

In 2011, Adrian lost in the central draw singles second round of the Wimbledon Open. In two years, Mannarino partook in the 2013 Wimbledon Championships and took the win over Pablo Andújar in the first round. 

Therefore, this took him to the single’s third round of a Grand Slam for the first time. He took over Dustin Brown and veteran Łukasz Kubot but lost to Jerzy Janowicz during the event.

ATP World Tour 

In 2017, Mannarino defeated two seeded players, Borna Ćorić ( first round) and Fernando Verdasco (quarterfinals), to advance to Antalun Open final but lost to Yuichi Sugita in the finals. 

He reached his first ATP World Tour Masters 1000 singles quarterfinal at the 2017 Rogers Cup. Adrian took down world No. 10 Milos Raonic; however, he lost to Denis Shapovalov.

After that, Mannarino gained his foremost career biggest singles win against Marlin Cilic, the biggest win of his career and his first win-win over a member of the Top 5 in the ATP singles rankings.

Later, he earned his first ATP World Tour 500 Series singles final in the Japan Open. 

The French tennis player played his first ATP World Tour tournament of 2018 at the Sydney International. After the event, he claimed his career-high of World no. 25 in the ATP singles rankings.

In 2019, Adrian won the 2019 Rosmalen Grass Court Championships by defeating Jordan Thompson in the final.

He started his 2021 season at the Delray Beach Open, where he lost in the second round to Cameron Norrie. Similarly, he retired during his first-round encounter against fifth seed Grigor Dimitrov in the Mexico Open.

Mannarino has recorded 191–226 (45.8%) in his singles match, while he has 27–75 (26.5%) in his double game. 

Adrian Mannarino | 2020 Incident 

In September 2020, Mannarino had a Grand Slam tennis match against No. 5 Alexander Zverev of Russia.

Their match was supposed to be held at Louis Armstrong Stadium; however, both of them didn’t show up for the game. 

Later Mannarino explained that the health officials had stated that he must remain quarantined in his hotel room for another week. 

He told the media, “It was a weird situation for me. I didn’t know what to think. I was just laying on the sofa, still trying to be focused, just in case I would go to court.”

The health officers clarified that Alexander and Adrian are kept quarantined as they came in contact with Benoit Paire, who tested Covid-19 on the eve of the tournament.

Ball Boy Story

During a Wimbledon event in 2017, Adrian Mannarino collided with a ball boy during his second-round win over Japan’s Yūichi Sugita.

Following the collision, the Serbian umpire Marijana Veljovic gave Mannarino “unsportsmanlike conduct.”

That was when Mannarino had a statement that pulled out an array of controversies surrounding the athlete. Thus, the umpire stated Mannarino’s behavior was unacceptable. 

He said, “It’s a joke. I’m hurting myself just not to hurt [him], and you give me a warning. Ball kids are the priority, right? Can I not walk to my chair? I’m hitting him as much as he’s hitting me. I don’t know who has priority on the court. Is it the players or the ball boys? Can you do Wimbledon with just ball boys? I don’t know.”

He later told French media that he didn’t walk into the ball boy on purpose despite what the Serbian umpire believed.
