Jeff Hardy | Early Life, Family & Education

The WWE Star, Jeff Hardy, was born on August 31, 1997, in Cameron, North Carolina, the United States.

His father is Gilbert, and his mother, Ruby Moore Hardy. Jeff grew up with his older brother, Matt Hardy. Young Jeff lost his mother to brain cancer when he was just nine years old.

He has been looked after by his father ever since the passing of his mother. Jeff Hardy used to work on a tobacco farm to sustain the needs of his family.

Moreover, Jeff joined the Union Pines High School. He was also a part of the football team during his school year. At age 13, he played as a fullback and a linebacker as well.

Besides, Jeff was a bike fanatic. Hardy joined his first racing event when he was in ninth grade.

He had an accident in a motocross race and had to stop racing because of his injury. Moreover, he also enjoyed playing baseball in high school.

Furthermore, during his high school year, he also competed in amateur wrestling. As a result, he stopped playing football and pursued his passion for wrestling.

Jeff Hardy’s first competition took place in 1994 against the infamous Razor Ramon.

Moreover, before joining WWE, Jeff and his brother Hardy founded the trampoline wrestling federation.

Jeff Hardy | Career

Before Fame

As a wrestling enthusiast, Jeff Hardy used to see The Ultimate Warrior, Shan Michaels, and Hulk Hogan as his inspirations.

Jeff started his career at WWF as a jobber. A jobber is someone who consistently loses to make the opponent look stronger.

After a short time, Jeff Hardy signed a contract with WWF in 1993 at 16. Gary Sabaugh suggested he and his brother to Tony Garea, who was an agent himself.

Jeff Hardy’s match against Razor Ramon took place in Youngstown, Ohio. Randy Savage was on commentary on the night of the event.

Keith Davis was the scheduled jobber for Razor that night, who backed out on short notice.

Hardy Boyz

Before establishing themselves in WWF, Jeff and Matt had formed their wrestling promotion with Thomas Simpson. It was known as OMEGA or Organization of Modern Extreme Grappling Art.

The World Wrestling Federation finally paid attention to the Hardy Brothers. Shortly after. They signed a contract in 1998 and trained with Dory Funk Jr.

The Hardy brothers continued training at the Dojo with Christian, Kurt Angle, A-train, and Test. Later down the line, Jeff Hardy and his brother finally formed a tag team together.

The WWF audience recognized them as the “Brood.” The Hardy brothers used to initiate feuds against the likes of Christian and Edge.

Jeff and his brother Matt also won the prestigious World Tag Team Championship during their reign in the WCW.

Solo career

Jeff Hardy also had a successive solo career as a wrestler. As a singles wrestler, he won the world championship six times.

Likewise, he has also held World Heavyweight Championship twice, TNA World Heavyweight Championship thrice, and the WWE title once.

Moreover, he has won the Intercontinental Championship four times(WWE) and the European Championship once.

During his career, Jeff received a significant boost in WWE as he was challenged for the WWE championship in 2009 at the Royal Rumble.

Furthermore, he went on to win the WWE championship in 2008 at Armageddon pay-per-view.

Jeff Hardy | Controversies

Jeff Hardy made a debut for Ring of Honor Wrestling debut in early 2003. A year later, in 2004, WWE conducted a drug test, and Hardy came positive.

It led him to be discarded by the storyline and dismissed by the WWE. After WWE dismissed him, Jeff signed a contract with Total Nonstop Action Wrestling in 2004.

However, he rarely got to demonstrate his talents as the company faced numerous issues with Jeff.

For instance, Jeff Hardy would not appear for scheduled matches on numerous occasions. After his spell in TNA, he goes on to sign a contract with WWE (World Wrestling Federation).

During the same year, in October, Jeff wins the Intercontinental Title after his match against John Morrison or Nitro.

The wrestler engaged in a feud, which resulted in the title being changed back and forth.

In August 2007, Jeff failed a test by the official drug policy. It resulted in Jeff Hardy being suspended for 30 days by WWE.

However, Jeff Hardy’s suspension was lifted by WWE. Shortly after, he made his ring appearance against Mr. Kennedy. Furthermore, he goes on to win the Intercontinental title from Umaga.

WWE considered Jeff to be rebellious outside of the ring due to his drug problems. It led WWE to write off Jeff Hardy from the main storyline.

During the same time, Jeff announced his break from wrestling on his Twitter page. In addition, he explains to his fans his devotion to projects outside of the ring.

Comeback to TNA

Jeff Hardy made a surprise appearance on an issue of TNA on January 4, 2010. It most certainly proved rumors surrounding Jeff’s return and commitment to TNA.

TNA announced on January 5, 2010, that they had signed the contract with Jeff. Further, Hardy goes on to make a consistent appearance on the platform and loses to brother Matt Hardy.

Since his defeat with Matt Hardy, he appears under Brother Nero and serves his brother as an assistant.
