Jeopardy! fans divided over contestant who beat super-champ Ray Lalonde as he proves critics wrong w

Posted by Martina Birk on Sunday, June 2, 2024

JEOPARDY! contestant Lloyd Sy, who defeated super-champion Ray LaLonde, has proven not to be a one-trick pony.

He surprised Jeopardy! skeptics after taking out the fan favorite by winning his second game on Wednesday and a bundle of cash.

The 2-day champ faced Sarah Palmer, an executive assistant from Monterey, California and Francis Englert, a line cook from Scotch Plains, New Jersey.

Lloyd, a graduate student in literature from Rockford, Illinois took to the left podium.

Last evening, he beat 13-time champ Ray who had been in that slot for some time and amassed $386K while there.

Lloyd didn't have any trouble carrying the torch, however.

He nabbed an impressive $4K "Daily Double" and by Final Jeopardy had $20.2K to Francis' $14.2K and Sarah's $8.2K.

Host Ken Jennings, 48, remarked that he nearly had a "runaway."

Final Jeopardy under "Continental Geography" read: "Until a 1903 secession, this country's contiguous territory spanned 2 continents."

It was a bit of a confusing one but only Lloyd pieced together the clue was looking for North and South America.

He correctly wrote "What is: Columbia" with a unique wager of $8888.

Nonetheless, the long-haired newcomer was deemed a 2-day winner with $29K and $53K total and looked so proud.

'NOT A FLUKE!'

One Reddit user rejoiced: "I guess Lloyd beating Ray wasn't a fluke! Congrats to Lloyd!"

"I love Lloyd’s face when he won haha," wrote another viewer.

"The new guy is kind of wacky," a third fan harshly accused still getting used to not seeing the gentlemanly Ray.

A fourth wrote: "Really enjoy Lloyd’s personality. I love that he’s goofy and sincere and I think very genuinely a little uncomfortable with the focus being on him, but very comfortable with his knowledge."

RAY'S LAST DAY

Ray's loss on January 3rd to Lloyd was one for the books even though many viewers didn't want to see him go.

By Final Jeopardy, he was in second place with $12,000, whereas Lloyd had $12,490 and Claire Theoret $11,200.

It was quite a sight to see all three contestants in the five digits as host Ken remarked: "What a performance by all three players."

Under "Foreign-born authors" Final Jeopardy read: "Early in her career she translated romance novels into Spanish, often changing the dialogue to make the heroines smarter."

The correct response was "Who is Isabel Allende?"

Third placer Claire and Ray both bet their entire amounts and Ray had $24K to which Ken said: "You, sir, have the lead."

Lloyd covered his face before flashing a huge smile - he added $12,000, to win with $24,490 and end the big winner's streak.

Beating Ray by a mere $458 left no hard feelings on the board as they posed together for a snap outside the Jeopardy! studios.

"Me and the guy she told me not to worry about," Lloyd joked in his caption on Twitter, alluding to the upset victory.

The full body shot showed Ray in casual wear in a purple tee, glasses, and jeans - Ray will return for the 2023 Tournament of Champions. 

The TV set designer who recently wrapped on Hulu's Handmaid's Tale is now one of the show's 16 super-champions with 10 wins or more.

He is the first super-champ in his 50s and the second Canadian next to 23-time winner Mattea Roach and amassed the second-longest streak of this season after 21-time winner Cris Pannullo.

CURSE LIFTED?

Interestingly enough, Lloyd's second win is a rarity as many players lose one night after taking out a big winner.

Being a "Giant Killer" - or a contestant to do so - is actually considered something of a curse.

Out of the 16 contestants in Jeopardy! history to have won more than 10 games, at least seven of their victors didn't make it to a second win.

Those include Nancy Zerg who "H&R blocked" now-host Ken out of a 75th win and Cris Pannullo's recent victor whom Cris shaded as "lucky."

Over the summer, Ken and actress Mayim Bialik were announced as the permanent replacements for the late longtime host, Alex Trebek.
