What are early payout offers?

Early payout offers remain difficult to find online. As such, you may not be familiar with just how they work. This type of promotion means that bookies will pay out early when certain situations arise. For example, if your Premier League football pick goes 2-0 up in a match. 

Bets are settled before completion, with what happens in the remainder of the event not being important. Your selection could be pegged back to 2-2, but you will have already received your winnings. Bookies introduce this offer type in an attempt to grow their brand, with it being one of the most popular ongoing promotions for punters in the UK today. 

How do early payouts work?

When explaining how early payout offers work, the easiest thing to do is provide some examples. Early payout promos are most commonly associated with football betting. So, let’s suggest that you backed Arsenal to beat Chelsea in the FA Cup. Arsenal is winning 2-1 at half-time. At this point, your bet is still very much up in the air. However, the Gunners scored another goal shortly after the break, putting them 3-1 ahead. Due to your pick having moved two goals clear, your bet will be settled as a win immediately. 

Basketball is another sport that early payout offers can be used on. For example, those who follow the NBA may bet on the Golden State Warriors to beat the Boston Celtics in the Playoffs. The Warriors moved into a 20-point lead after a dominant third quarter. Given the rule of the early payout offer, your bet will be settled as a win instantly. As such, it doesn’t matter what the final result is.

When is the best time to cash out?

You may have recognized the link between early payout and cash out. However, the two are different. With an early payout offer, punters qualifying will receive 100% of their winnings. This is compared to cash out, where you take home a proportion of their potential earnings, based on what is offered by your chosen bookie. So, should you cash out your bet or wait for an early payout offer to kick in? Unfortunately, there is no right answer. 

It all depends on the event and what is taking place on the pitch, court, or track. For example, if you are confident of your football selection going two goals clear, it is worth waiting for an early payout, so that you can claim maximum winnings. However, if you are not convinced and the teams involved are a little more evenly matched than you anticipated, cash out should be used. Just consider timings, with time remaining and the current scoreline, as well as other factors, dictating how much bookies offer through cash out. 

Top tips for early payout offers

In order to get the most out of early payout offers, be sure to follow our expert tips, which are listed below:

  • Read the T&Cs - It is important to read the rules of early payout offers in full. While terms and conditions are generally in the favour of punters, you must know exactly what is required in order to qualify. 
  • Look for Fast Starters - While you should not look to qualify for an early payout in particular, it is always worthwhile looking for teams and players that start quickly. For example, some football teams are known for scoring goals within the opening 15 minutes of games.
  • Search for Value - This is the case with all bets, but remember to take your time and search for value. If you have a good knowledge of a sport and are prepared to do your homework then you will often find attractive odds. 
  • Cash Out or Early Payout - You will often be faced with the dilemma as to whether to cash out or wait for an early payout. So, be sure to keep a close eye on the amount being offered to cash out. 
  • Stick to What You Know - You should not place bets specifically in the hope of qualifying for an early payout. So, if an early payout offer is only available on football and you know little about this sport, focus on what you know.
