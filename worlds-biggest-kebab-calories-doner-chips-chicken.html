Worlds biggest kebab with 5,350 calories will leave you drooling all day The Sun

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Sunday, April 7, 2024

A TAKEAWAY shop owner claims to have put the world’s biggest kebab on the menu – but could you scoff all 5,350 calories of the monster feast?

The giant kebab features two large portions of chips, a large and small portion of doner meat, a large and small portion of chicken as well as cheese and curry sauce smothered over the top.

Customers have travelled far and wide to try the meat feast since its release  but owner of the Chip Inn, Nottinghamshire, Megan Altinoz, 33, says no-one has ever polished one off.

The kebab could feed a family of four and still have leftovers, but customers are invited to try it alone for £22.

Megan said: “A customer came in wanting a huge kebab, but we didn’t have a box big enough for it, so we used a 15-inch pizza box and that’s where this all started.

“We put it on our Facebook page and the comments exploded, everyone seemed to really like the look of it so we thought we should just put it on the menu.

“We’ve had customers from all over the UK come and try our challenge, but nobody has finished it to our knowledge yet.

“We have had pictures sent to us saying they manged to do it but we don’t know if they had help.

“The kebab is enormous, and the weight of it is terrifying, we struggle to even pick it up and pop it on the counter to be taken away.

“We have been open for over 20 years and it’s a family run business, our produce is fresh and homemade."

How the giant kebab stacks up

One large portion of doner meat - 1,000 calories

One small portion of doner meat - 600 calories

One large portion of chicken - 700 calories

One small portion of chicken - 500 calories

Two large portions of chips - 2,000 calories

Six pots of curry sauce - 600 calories

200g of grated cheese - 450 calories

Kebab fan William Lailey, 30, took on the challenge but admitted to The Sun Online he was soon a goner.

He said: "I failed miserably. I didn't eat all afternoon either. I gave it my best shot though. I think I got through about the equivalent of two large kebabs and chips."

At nearly 6,000 calories, the giant kebab is nearly three times the daily amount someone should have in one day.
