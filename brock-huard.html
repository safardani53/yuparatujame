Brock Huard is an American co-host on the sports radio talk show KIRO-AM, the Brock and Salk show, with co-host Mike Salk, and is a former football quarterback in the NFL. He last played for the Seattle Seahawks.

The former NFL quarterback played football for 6 years with Indianapolis Colts and Seattle Seahawks.

Huard’s whole family has been linked with the sports world. From his father to his siblings, all of them were involved in football.

Huard’s older brother Damon also had a career in the NFL, and his younger brother Luke played for North Carolina and pursued a coaching career.

As a color commentator, Brock has worked for sports channels like ESPN and Fox Sports Network. Brock Huard called in NFL games for FOX alongside Chris Myers, Greg Jennings, and reporter Jen Hale.

Brock Huard | Early Life

Fully named Brock Anthony Huard, he was born on April 15, 1976, to his parents, Peggy Huard and Mike Huard. His birthplace is Seattle, Washington, and he falls under the Aries zodiac sign.

He is the middle child among three children. He has an older brother, Damon Huard, and a younger one named Luke Huard. Even though the Huard family isn’t the first to have been involved in sports as a whole.

Brock’s father, Mike Huard, was born on Jan 23, 1950, in Prosser and enrolled in Prosser High School. As a youth, he played football, basketball, and baseball and was renowned for being a high school’s three-sport standout.

However, moving to Central Washington University, he starred in football. By the end of the graduation, he had bagged All-Evergreen Conference, all-district, and honorable mention NAIA West Coast honors.

Afterward, he commenced his coaching career through White Swan High School and retired in the 1997 season. Then, he began as a physical education teacher at Puyallup High School.

“Love your kids, enjoy every day, give special thanks to your wives because, without them, you wouldn’t be what you are, and be kind to people.”
-Mike Huard

Mother, Peggy Huard

Peggy Huard is a Dutch-born in the Netherlands who eventually moved to the United States, graduating from Enumclaw High School in 1969.

She worked for the Puyallup School District in the areas of special education.

Peggy dated Mike Huard and became a footballer mother, busy at home raising the kids.

Older Brother, Damon Huard

Damon Huard is the family’s eldest child, born on July 9, 1973, in Yakima. He attended Puyallup High School and moved to the University of Washington, where he majored in business.

He is an NFL player who first commenced his play in Germany and later played beside Tom Brady. Damon is married to his wife, Julie, and they are parents to Holly, Sam, and Brooke.

As of 2022, he is the director of community relations and fundraising for the University of Washington football program, his alma mater.

Younger Brother, Luke Huard

Luke Huard is the youngest child of the Huard family, born on Oct 1, 1979, in Redmond.

Like his brothers, he graduated from the same school and the University of North Carolina with a political science degree.

Moreover, Luke played football parallel to his studies and later focused on coaching part.

Luke joined the USC staff in February of 2022 as an offensive analyst.

Brock Huard Education

Huard enrolled in the same school as his father and brother. After graduating, he attended the University of Washington, majoring in psychology.

Brock Huard maintained an impressive 3.6 GPA in his college.

Puyallup High School

Brock Huard was the left-handed quarterback in high school as he led his father’s team.

Playing under his father’s coaching, Huard bagged many accolades, including the Gatorade National Player of the Year, All-State, and Class AAA State Player-of-the-Year.

His other wins were Parade, Super Prep, Blue Chip Illustrated, ESPN, College Sports, and Schutt All-America. He had also featured in basketball as he posted 18.1 points and 7.5 rebounds as a senior.

As a footballer, Huard had recorded 237 passes, 45 touchdowns, and 10 interceptions.

Coming out of high school, he was the top sought athlete in colleges. He had listed UCLA and Washington as his choices and later committed to Washington.

University Career

Brock Huard commenced his college days as a redshirt freshman and competed against the Mukilteo’s Shane Fortney for the starting position.

As Fortney was selected as the starter, Huard had his foremost career action in the 1996 season at Arizona State.

At first, Huard played behind senior quarterback Jake Plummer, and as Fortney was injured, he took his position as the starter.

Then, Fortney transferred to Northern Iowa, and Huard led Washington to fourth place in the AP poll.

Following the 1997 season, he missed the games due to his ankle injury. After returning in the 1998 season, he commenced his gameplay through a win at Arizona State.

During his tenure, Huard surpassed numerous records of his older brother.

By the end of his time frame, Huard had 20 school records. His stats were 5,742 passing yards, 51 touchdown passes, 151 attempts without an interception, and 191.4 total yards per game.

Brock was named Academic All-American in his final two seasons.

He was the finalist for the Davey O’Brien National Quarterback Award and was also All-Pac-10 honors as a sophomore at the University of Washington.

Brock Huard | Professional Career

After honorable graduation from Washington, Brock Huard entered the 1999 NFL Draft. The Seattle Seahawks signed Huard as the 77th overall pick in the third round.

Apparently, he was the seventh quarterback drafted by the Seahawks by the new general manager and head coach Mike Holmgren.

Seattle Seahawks

Brock Huard’s foremost professional game came in 2000 as the backup to Jon Kitna. Before it, he was the third-string, whose first pass attempt in the NFL preseason games was a touchdown.

Starring behind Kitna, Huard played his first game against the Dolphins. Later, he even started four games where he recorded three touchdowns and two interceptions.

In 2000, when Brock stood against the Denver Broncos, his older brother was against the Indianapolis Colts. Thus, they became the first set of brothers in NFL history to start at quarterback on the same weekend.

Following it, he started game six, which unfortunately bought an injury two games later. Huard struggled with a kidney injury against the Broncos and only appeared in one game.

Indianapolis Colts

As the Seattle Seahawks took in Matt Hasselbeck, they traded Brock Huard to the Indianapolis Colts in 2002. During his tenure with the Colts, he appeared as the backup for Peyton Manning.

Subsequently, Huard signed to be a third-string quarterback for the Seahawks. Unfortunately, he spent the whole season on the injured list, ending his professional career.

Overall, Brock Huard’s NFL career stats stand as  60 of 109 for 689 yards with 4 touchdowns and 2 interceptions with a quarterback rating of 80.3.

Brock Huard | After NFL

Huard commenced his broadcasting career in 2008 through ESPN radio, where he covered college football networks for a decade.

Additionally, he has experience with ESPN, ABC, and the SEC Network.

He then worked as the color analyst with play-by-play announcer Bob Wischusen. Together, they covered ESPN college football broadcasts in the SEC and Big Ten.

Later, Huard stood as the color commentator for the 2013 Seahawks preseason games. During it, he worked alongside Fox Sports’ Curt Menefee as they did play-by-play on KCPQ and KZJO.

Before joining Fox Sports in 2019, Huard had jobs announcing for ESPN Pop Warner football specials live from Disney’s Wide World of Sports.

He is a co-host on a sports radio talk show KIRO-AM and the Brock and Salk show with co-host Mike Salk.

The Brock and Salk radio show on ESPN 710 Seattle moved to the podcast only in late September 2019.

On the podcast, Huard said Seahawks’ Tariq Woolen would prove he’s a shutdown CB vs. Mahomes on 24 December 2022. As Seattle Seahawks faced defeat to the Chiefs in Kansas City. 

Brock Huard said on Seattle Sports’, “He had 33 coverages snaps, he was targeted twice, and he knocked down one ball, once again showing unbelievable makeup speed.”

On 27 December 2022, Huard commented on how the Seahawks can get their offense back on track.

He stated that the Seahawks had a slower pace of play in Kansas City, and the Chiefs’ defense was ‘messing around,’ which could mean it could alter the sight that Seattle’s offense was seeing on the other side of the line of scrimmage.

Seattle may need to amp up its pace to stop that from getting in the way of its offense again in a must-win game against the Jets.

Brock Huard And Jim Mora

The story between Brock Huard and Jim Mora dates back to 2017 when the former Washington QB Brock Huard was one of ESPN’s broadcasters. Back then, he was hosting at Husky Stadium for the UCLA game against Washington.

Of course, it was also when UCLA quarterback Josh Rosen suffered an apparent left-hand injury. In addition, Huard had covered the scene stating if anything was going on with Josh and if the number one quarterback had a wrong turn. 

Later, reporters had asked Jim Mora about those questioning Rosen’s ‘toughness.’ That was when Mora went on with his statement stating it wasn’t Huard’s opinion that was needed. Also, his statement would only bring up controversial ground.

“I think it’s ludicrous that anyone would question Josh Rosen’s toughness when they don’t know the nature of his injury. Maybe the producer was talking in his ear, I don’t know.”

“But it doesn’t make any sense to me that Brock Huard has any way to ever question Josh Rosen. But that’s his job, so I do understand that that’s his job.”

Indeed, Brock Huard also made his piece of statement afterward.

“He’s going to protect his team. He’s going to do whatever he can to protect his player. If that means taking some shots at me, that was in his prerogative to do so.”
