Who are Keyshia Cole's sisters Elite Noel and Neffeteria Pugh?

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Wednesday, March 27, 2024

FAMILY members have paid tribute on social media to Keyshia Cole’s mom Frankie Lons who died after a drugs overdose.

Keyshia's two sisters were among those who posted heartbreaking messages after Frankie's death.

Who are Keyshia Cole's sisters?

Elite Noel

Elite is Keyshia's younger sister.

She was born in July 1985 in California.

Like her sister, she is also in the music industry as she is a singer, songwriter, and rapper - she released her single BAE in 2017.

Elite has also featured on the show Keyshia Cole: All In.

The star has over 85,000 followers on Instagram and she mainly shares updates of projects she has been working on.

In her bio, she describes herself as the Co-CEO of the cosmetics brand Klub Kosmetics and the founder of the page #FuturisticSoul.

During an episode of BET reality TV show Frankie & Neffe, Elite and her sister Neffeteria took a DNA test to see if they shared the same father.

However, the test revealed that Neffe's father Ellis "Vic" Randolph was not Elite's father.

Elite was adopted at 3 months old and did not find out she was adopted until age 11.

In December 2020 she posted a photo with her adoptive dad with an emotional caption: ''Even tho I was raised by an amazing man ( my adopted dad) u didn’t hesitate to call me your daughter."

Neffeteria Pugh

Neffeteria or “Neffe“ is Keyshia's older sister.

She was born in December 1979 in California.

Her father is Ellis “Vic” Randolph.

She describes herself as an executive producer, an author, and an actress on her Instagram bio.

Pugh is best known as one of the two co-stars of the reality documentary series, the Frankie and Neffe Show, along with Frankie Lons.

She wrote My Happiness Is My Sanity in 2009 a few years later in 2012, The Price I Paid.

Both books are narrating the challenges of living with a celebrity sister and handling the stress of family and her professional career.

Neffe is a mom-of-five, Brianna, Ciara, JayLann, Nayla, and Star.

What have they said about their mom's death?

Elite Noel was the first to announce the news of her mom's death in an Instagram post.

Elite wrote: “Worse pain ever. To see my mama in a body bag on her birthday! My heart so f***** broke."
