Meet the 'Elon Musk of the tiny home movement' who lives in a $20,000 pint-sized abode that's only 8

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Sunday, March 31, 2024

THIS tiny home company founder lives in a 16 foot house with his dog.

Randy Jones founded Incredible Tiny Homes in Tennessee, and showed the property that he lives in, on YouTube.

This home is eight by sixteen feet and costs $20,000.

The house is covered in painted panels on the outside and decorated with a bright green door and wooden trims.

His home is part of their Incred-I Line of tiny homes, which comes as either sixteen feet or twenty feet.

Randy has lived in this home for almost two years now.

The home includes a bed, kitchen, shower, toilet, and even a bed for Randy’s dog.

Randy said: “I can sit, I can watch my TV, I can lay in bed, I’ve got room to cook and clean, I can get on the floor, I can work out a little bit, I’ve got room for his [pet dog] bed."

He even has a metal tub for ice baths and a grill outside his home.

Randy added: “All these things are what you have in a big house it’s just how you wrap your head around it.”

Most read in Money

He expressed that demand for his twenty feet homes has surpassed the sixteen feet homes but Randy still lives in his sixteen feet space.

Randy said: “I’m glad you’ve come over here because we forget about these 16 foot homes.

"Since we’ve come out with our 20 foot homes everybody wants a 20 footer.

“It is pretty much 95% the same the only thing we have tweaked a little bit is our backsplash that we are doing, our plugs, and different things.

“Bedroom, kitchen, bathroom, closet, storage you literally have everything you need in this 16 foot house, I have everything I need.”

“Everything in here is about efficiency and cost.”

Randy even discussed how he put up shelves and decorations on the walls.

He said: “People ask, how do you attach things on the walls?

“If I take wood I put liquid nail and I set a couple screws.

“The screw only has to go through the metal, that’s it, and the metal is only a sixteenth of an inch big.”

Randy then showed how his method is strong enough to hold his TV up on the wall too.

He also expressed that this tiny home can be very versatile for different groups of people.

Randy said: “If you’ve got an elderly person that may need a home, a young person, even a couple could live in here.”
