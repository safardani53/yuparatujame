Fruity Pebbles launches two brand new products - but shoppers must look in a different aisle to grab

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Sunday, April 21, 2024

THOSE who enjoy their breakfasts extra sweet can now indulge in some brand new Fruity Pebbles meals.

The new Pebbles Waffles come in two different flavors, Fruity and Cocoa just like the signature cereal blends.

“Pebbles cereal fans are always looking for new ways to experience their favorite cereal,” said Joe Christenson, senior brand manager of Pebbles, in a statement.

“We’ve seen fans get creative by mixing Pebbles cereal into waffles and other breakfast recipes, so we were inspired to launch Pebbles Waffles in collaboration with Bob Evans Farms for a fun, easy way to enjoy the iconic flavour of Pebbles cereal.”

Each frozen waffle treat comes infused with Fruity or Cocoa pebbles and has a buttermilk batter base.

Currently, customers can score a six-count of the treat for less than $5 at most grocery stores.

And, in what is even better news for shoppers, Pebbles is celebrating the release by giving fans a free taste of the New Pebbles Waffles.

All you have to do is go to your local retailer to purchase either one of the flavors and then upload your receipt to getfreewaffles.com.

You’ll then get an automatic rebate via PayPal or Venmo for up to one box of waffles.

The freebie deal officially goes live on August 21, otherwise known as National Waffle Day.

BRAND NEW BREAKFAST EATS

Shoppers have plenty of other options if they’re not feeling a Fruity Pebbles-infused waffle for their first meal of the day.

Just last week, General Mills introduced its Pumpkin Spice Cheerios as a limited edition fall flavor.

The new Cheerios are made with real pumpkin puree and a blend of cinnamon, cloves, and nutmeg.

Meanwhile, popular wafflemaker Eggo has launched what it’s calling the Eggo Brunch in a Jar Sippin' Cream.

The breakfast-flavored liqueur is only available in certain liquor stores across the United States, but shoppers are already excited to try the unique alcohol.

According to the Sugarlands website, the Eggo Brunch in a Jar Sippin' Cream tastes like "toasted Eggo waffles, rich maple syrup, and notes of creamy butter, with a savory hint of bacon."

OTHER NEW PRODUCTS

Plenty of other food companies have taken advantage of the summer season and passing holidays to launch brand new versions of your favorite snacks and treats.

Lays, for instance, unveiled a new lineup of chip flavors inspired by the popular Flavor Swap potato chips.

In Canada, the Ruffles All Dressed has ruled the potato chip market for a while now, and Lays believe the new American chip will be an “extraordinary new flavor experience certain to be an instant international sensation,” according to the press release.

The company refers to the new chip as “a tangy, savory and sweet flavor experience all in one bite,” according to the release.

Truly, a popular hard seltzer company, has also unveiled a brand new summer item and custom flavor, Orange Slices.

The flavor is available in custom 16 ounce four packs and will be available while supplies last during the U.S. women’s soccer tournament.

Each drink has hints of orange and other citrus flavors and a “light, bright, refreshing experience wrapped in team spirit that takes drinkers back to simpler times on the soccer field and big dreams for a W,” Truly said in its press release.

McDonald’s also unveiled a new McFlurry flavor, the heavily anticipated Peanut Butter Crunch.

The ice cream dessert officially went live last week and comes with McDonald's signature creamy vanilla soft serve, crispy cereal mix and chocolatey peanut butter cookie pieces.
