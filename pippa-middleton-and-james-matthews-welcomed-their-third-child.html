Pippa Middleton And James Matthews Welcomed Their Third Child

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Thursday, March 14, 2024

Pippa Middleton, Kate Middleton’s younger sister, just delivered her third child with husband James Matthews. The couple also has a three-year-old boy named Arthur and a one-year-old daughter named Grace. According to People, the socialite-journalist gave birth to a baby girl a few weeks ago at St. Mary’s Hospital’s Lindo Wing, where the Duchess of Cambridge previously welcomed all three of her children. Middleton’s pregnancy was initially revealed in June when she wore a green dress to London’s “Party at the Palace” performance in honor of Queen Elizabeth II’s Platinum Jubilee.

Her spouse was there, as were her brother James Middleton, his wife Alizee Thevenet, and her parents Carole and Michael Middleton. Kate Middleton and Prince William were also there, as were their oldest children, Prince George, and Princess Charlotte. According to a source close to Pippa Middleton, the socialite intends to “have children and live a tranquil life” with her husband.

The couple is also expected to relocate to a hamlet near Bucklebury, Berkshire, with their children, according to the magazine. The property is said to be 50 minutes from Windsor Castle, where the Duke and Duchess of Cambridge will be relocating this summer.

Everything you need to know about Pippa Middleton’s job and lifestyle.

Pippa Middleton is a well-known British socialite, journalist, and author. She is the younger sister of Kate Middleton, Duchess of Cambridge, and was born to Carole and Michael Middleton. Her father worked as a flight controller for British Airways, while her mother was a flight attendant. Middleton is from a rich Yorkshire family affiliated with the British aristocracy, and her father is from a wealthy Yorkshire family linked with the British nobility. Pippa Middleton began her schooling at St Andrew’s School in Pangbourne before moving on to Downe House School in Cold Ash. She then attended Marlborough College on a sports or all-rounder scholarship before graduating from the University of Edinburgh with a degree in English literature.

She also has a master’s degree in Physical Education, Sport, and Physical Literacy from the University of Wales Trinity Saint David. Middleton initially came to public attention as the maid of honor during her sister’s wedding to Prince William in 2011. Following her education, Middleton began working at a luxury goods PR business in 2008 before landing a position in event management with the London-based company Table Talk. Tatler Magazine called her “the Number One Society Singleton” the same year.

Career

Pippa Middleton began writing columns for newspapers such as The Spectator in 2012. In 2013, she also contributed to a culinary column in Waitrose Kitchen magazine before being designated Vanity Fair’s contributing editor. She also started writing a biweekly sports and social section for The Sunday Telegraph that year. She also worked part-time at Party Pieces, where she edited the Party Times online magazine. Pippa left her literary agency the next year to become the director and shareholder of her own firm, PXM Enterprises Limited. In 2017, the firm was closed down.

Middleton became an ambassador for the British Heart Foundation in 2014. In 2016, she published her second book, Heartfelt, and donated the proceeds to charity. Middleton was also engaged to hedge fund manager and former racing driver James Matthews in 2016 and married in 2017. Matthews is the heir to the Scottish medieval title of Laird of Glen Affric, which encompasses a 10,000-acre estate in Scotland.
