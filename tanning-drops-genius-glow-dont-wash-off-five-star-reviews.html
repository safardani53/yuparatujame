TANNING fans are raving about a facial serum which gives them the perfect glow – without needing to be washed off.

Applying fake tan can be a long and messy business, and can all sympathise with staining our sheets, getting streaks or spending ages in the shower scrubbing stubborn patches.

But a facial tanning serum, from Tropic, promises a streak-free application without the need to wash off the product.

The Sun Drops Gradual Tanning Facial Serum, which costs £22, has received dozens of five-star reviews online.

Simply add a few drops to your normal moisturiser, apply to your face and then you’re ready to head out.

Describing the time-saving serum, Tropic said: “Just a few drops added to your daily moisturiser will gift skin with a radiant, sun-kissed glow.

“Packed with patented tanning technology and a cocktail of skin-brightening plant and fruit extracts, this nourishing serum illuminates skin as you tan for a natural looking, streak-free result.”

The tan will gradually develop, reaching its full colour after ten hours.

But you can tailor how dark you want to be, with some shoppers saying they used less in summer.

Tropic said: “For gorgeously radiant skin, mix a few drops with your daily moisturiser.

“Mix 2-3 drops for a subtle change in skin tone or 4-6 drops for a more sun-kissed glow.”

Shoppers have given it the seal of approval online, with one woman hailing it as “genius”.

One person commented: “Fantastic product. Gives my Casper complexion a sunkissed glow without turning Trump-orange. Works on legs and arms too. Love it!!”

Another wrote: “This stuff is genius! I just add a couple of drops to my moisturiser and apply before I go to bed and wake up with a really natural glow!

“It's so good because you can add an extra drop or 2 if you want more of a tanned look.”

A third said: “I adore this product, non greasy ad easy to use. Sun drops gives a very natural colour with no streaks!”

While this person added: “Perfect even colour and had so many compliments from my friends who thought I had been away on holiday.”
