Fans are 'distraught' after Pop Tart confirms customers will never see popular discontinued flavor a

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Sunday, May 5, 2024

POP Tart lovers have been horrified to find out their favorite breakfast pastry has been removed from stores for good.

Ever since Pop Tarts first showed up in stores in the 1990s, shoppers have craved the staple breakfast item.

While the product line originally only carried the Frosted Strawberry and Brown Sugar Cinnamon flavors, Pop Tart has expanded to provide every sort of flavor imaginable to its fans.

That included the iconic Blue Raspberry flavor, which featured sweet raspberry filling with purple and blue frosting.

When the Pop Tart mysteriously vanished from store shelves, shoppers prompted the brand for an explanation.

“Where is the blue raspberry poptart I'm distraught I miss it so much please bring it back I beg you,” one fan wrote on Twitter.

Read more about discontinued items

Pop Tarts’ Twitter account stepped in to confirm the beloved flavor had been discontinued, and it doesn’t seem like there’s any plans for it to come back.

“Bad news: It was discontinued,” the company replied. “Good news: we have lots of other flavors.”

Pop Tart currently offers 22 different flavors, ranging from old standbys like Frosted Chocolate Fudge to more unique flavors like Snickerdoodle and Frosted Grape.

The newest Pop Tarts to hit store shelves include Frosted Banana Bread, Apple Jacks Frosted Apple Cinnamon as well as the seasonal Red, White and Blueberry Pop Tarts.

However, despite the wide range of options, some fans remain committed to the beloved Blue Raspberry flavor.

“It’s the best flavor hands down,” a shopper wrote on a Reddit thread.

ANOTHER FALLEN POP TART

This isn’t the first time the Pop Tart brand has received backlash for a controversial product discontinuation.

Previously, the Cinnamon Roll-flavored Pop Tarts were also ripped from shelves, and shoppers remain upset about the sudden disappearance.

Each breakfast pastry boasted a gooey cinnamon-flavored filling surrounded by a golden, buttery crust.

On top, the sweet snack was finished with a signature swirl of cinnamon and an additional drizzle of white frosting.

Since the disappearance, fans rushed to the cinnamon flavored breakfast pastry’s defense, and many are begging to see the item come back.

"Cinnamon roll pop tarts really the best ones,” one fan wrote on Twitter.

Another one wrote: "Bring back Cinnamon Roll pop tarts!"

"I'm beggining you to start making Cinnamon Roll pop tarts again please I can't do this anymore,” said yet another disappointed shopper.

MORE DISCONTINUED TREATS

Shoppers have been noticing other popular items disappear from store aisles in recent months.

At Aldi, a popular brand of crunch wraps was discontinued from the store, and shoppers aren’t quite sure why.

The Reduced Sodium Whole Grain Wraps were enjoyed by customers looking for a healthier alternative, but they mysteriously vanished one day.

"It’s been months since I’ve seen these wraps at @AldiUSA did you guys stop selling them?” one shopper wrote on Twitter.

Aldi replied with some bad news.

"Sorry to disappoint, but they have been discontinued," the company responded.

Kroger has also felt backlash from customers after discontinuing its Turkey Hill ice cream.

The Turkey Hill Dairy brand was top-tier for many Kroger shoppers, offering an array of flavors from Black Cherry and Chocolate Chip Cookie Dough to Butter Pecan and Orange Cream Swirl.

One upset fan called out Kroger on Twitter.

“@kroger discontinued @TurkeyHillDairy ice cream. @kroger you are officially dead to me. Private Label is awful!” they wrote.

Meanwhile, at fast food giant Wendy’s, customers are threatening to riot if the discontinued bourbon bacon burger doesn’t come back to stores.

Each burger featured a square beef patty, topped with layers of bacon, melted cheese, crispy onions, and bourbon sauce.

Since the burger’s disappearance, fans have been fuming.

Under a recent Instagram post, one commented: “Day 20 of asking for the bourbon bacon burger to come back or I riot.”

“Bring it back, or else,” another upset customer wrote.
