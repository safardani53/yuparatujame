Sams Club confirms discontinued popular item - but theres hope for fans who want it back on th

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Monday, January 1, 0001

SAM’S Club has discontinued a popular item, sending fans scrambling for ways to bring it back.

And the company revealed there may be some hope for shoppers who want to see it on the shelves again

Sam’s Club confirmed during the week that it no longer sells Pom Wonderful fresh pomegranate arils.

The product was sold as a two-pack of eight-ounce cups which made handy snacks and healthy toppings.

One customer, Stephanie Ozuna, recently tweeted Sam’s Club to complain she had not seen the item at her local store.

“Please bring back the pomegranate two-pack cups. It’s been two months,” the shopper wrote, adding a crying emoji.

Sam’s Club replied: “We loved them as much as you did, Stephanie.”

The retailer pointed the customer in the direction of an online form that shoppers can use to give feedback, including requests for the return of discontinued items. 

“Done, thank you!” wrote Stephanie. 

However, Sam’s Club isn’t the only store that has recently scrapped some fan-favorite products.

OTHER DISCONTINUED ITEMS

Walmart let down fans as it confirmed it would not bring back Grapette grape soda.

Disappointed customers on Twitter claimed the drink was “one of the best,” although the retailer does still sell alternative brands of grape soda.

Kroger shoppers were also recently sad to learn the chain no longer sells The Fizzicist real sugar soda.

This store-brand alternative to Dr. Pepper was described by customers as their “favorite” soda and begged Kroger to “come back out with it again soon.”

The announcement followed customer outrage when Kroger decided to stop selling bags of Private Selection Sweet Southern Heat barbecue kettle chips.

WHAT YOU CAN DO

If your favorite item was recently discontinued, most retailers have an online contact form where you can request to bring it back.

Whether or not the product becomes available again may depend on whether the store can restock due to demand or if the original manufacturer has stopped producing it.

Grocery stores aren’t the only brands that have recently changed their offerings.
