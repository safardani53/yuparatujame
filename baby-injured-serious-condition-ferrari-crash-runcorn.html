Baby in serious condition after horror Ferrari crash as cops appeal for witnesses

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Thursday, September 19, 2024

A BABY is in serious condition in hospital after a horror smash involving a Ferrari.

Medics rushed the three-week-old baby, who was a passenger in a Ford Fiesta, to hospital on Friday night.

It comes after emergency services were scrambled to A558 Daresbury Expressway in Runcorn after getting frantic calls about a two-car crash.

The Ferrari driver, a 34-year-old man, was also raced to hospital with serious injuries and later discharged.

Today, police confirmed the baby remains in hospital in a serious condition.

Police are urging witnesses to come forward as they probe the smash.

Cheshire Police Sgt Andy Dennison said: "We're keen to speak to anyone who witnessed the collision or events leading up to it.

"The same goes for anyone with dash cam footage that may have captured events or prior driving of either vehicle leading up to the collision and the collision itself."

Anyone with information should contact Cheshire Police on 101 or via cheshire.police.uk/tell-us quoting IML 1601942.
